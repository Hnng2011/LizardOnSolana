The term "lizard" refers to a large and diverse group of reptiles belonging to the order Squamata. Lizards are characterized by their scaly skin, clawed toes, and external ear openings. Lizards exhibit a wide range of sizes, from tiny species that are just a few centimeters long to larger ones that can exceed several feet in length. They also have different diets, with some being herbivores, others omnivores, and some being carnivores. Lizards play important roles in ecosystems, contributing to pest control by feeding on insects and other small invertebrates.
